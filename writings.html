The hard life of a perfectionist
To be an imperfectionist being a perfectionist is rather difficult. The fences have already been built around, to confine one in the perfectionist zone. Every step has to be carefully planned owing to the fact that everything is judged. The only thing that traps you all along, is to be a perfectionist.

Yet, everyone has a desire to be their own perfectionist, keeping aside others’ perspectives. Others’ ‘being-imperfectionist’ conceptions might be one's perfectionist traits. Given about a 100 years to live, making most of it is what lingers into the brain. To love, to be loved, to care, to be cared for, to help, to be helped, to dream, and to achieve conquers your heart and brain. Being a perfectionist doesn’t matter anymore; being yourself does. Hardwired thoughts, myths, and societal pressure can crumple that idea; nonetheless, picking tiny moments of what’s left might satiate you. You make mistakes, and you learn to tell yourself that everything will be okay. You learn to be fearless, and you learn to make the most of what’s there with you. You understand the value of things that you otherwise wouldn’t have considered. It’s hard, it screws you up, but being a perfectionist can destroy you. You could never be the one you always wanted to. Just doing what you love and being the way you are, wouldn’t be too far to go after. However difficult they are, life isn’t what it is without them.
The beauty of being you
Unfurling the haphazardly tied threads of emotions seemed complicated to accomplish. A myriad of internal and external factors most probably have cast their evil spells on it. The repercussions thus followed, were insurmountable.

When a situation puts on an ugly face, emotions tend to take an excruciating diabolic form. They hate to disguise themselves from then on, and blatantly put forth the melancholia. None adore it; nevertheless, it’s inevitable. Everything slips away from the impenetrable obstacles, despite that, the heaviness of the inexplicable weight of emotions surges. The motionless body walks and runs. The hunger pangs pester, but that’s extraneous. Each passing day counts as worthless. There’s nothing more to do. Everything’s done and dusted. Period.

As days pass by, emotions quarrel among themselves. Hormonal imbalance dominates neurological functioning. The quest for stability takes its form. Brooding turns to brainstorming, and reality starts sinking in. Criticism and dominance aren’t part of the play anymore. It’s just you all the way! You matter the most to yourself. Anything you do, the rationale matters to only ‘you’. You hunt for the hidden soul to define yourself blazingly quick. You crave to prove your words and actions. The morals and principles you follow, stand by you all along. Taking the flak is of no concern, and you constantly endeavor to do what you love!
Thus, the threads have been unfurled.
The unwanted thrust into the dark abyss
Everything seems blissful on the flat surface we’re standing upon. We laugh with no regrets holding us back, we babble about an endless number of things, and we work to make the world a better place. The road we’re traveling upon is smooth.

Out of the blue, a terrible storm comes out of nowhere, and transports us, rather formally, into an abyss. The unbeknownst agony finds its way out through a labyrinth of heart rhythms. The wound is unhealable. Isolation is what we go after. Everyone keeps us far away from the souls we’re emotionally stuck to, but none pushes the two sides closer.
Nonetheless, we seek to travel alone. The spirit dampeners shower all along the line, we lick the sweet-and-sour sauce and pursue our goals. The distance, however, doesn’t relinquish and continually takes on the winning side. This further crumples the few remaining lively chunks of soul and thrusts us deeper into the dark abyss.
